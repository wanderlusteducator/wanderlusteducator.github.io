Academic Background

I hold a Master’s in Biology from the University of Rochester and a Bachelor's in Science (Research) with a major in Biology and minor in Physics from the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore. My research work spans areas of synthetic biology, biofabrication, and sustainability. During my academic years, I co-authored scientific publications and worked on cutting-edge interdisciplinary projects.

Teaching Experience

  • Graduate Teaching Assistant at University of Rochester: Introductory Biology & Computational Biology Labs
  • IGCSE Chemistry and Biology Tutor: Concept-focused, interdisciplinary, and student-driven approach
  • Designed and led a Critical Thinking & Scientific Wonder course blending science, literature, and philosophy

Outreach & Alternative Education

I trained in Waldorf Early Childhood Education and volunteered with the Rochester Education Justice Initiative, teaching incarcerated individuals. These experiences have shaped my belief in the transformative and inclusive power of education.

Beyond the Classroom

My journey has taken me across 20+ countries, often solo and on a budget. Travel has enriched my perspective, deepened my appreciation for cultural diversity, and reinforced my view that learning happens everywhere — from forests and films to folk songs and scientific journals.
