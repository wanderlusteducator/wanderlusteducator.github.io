How I Teach

My teaching is Socratic, dialectical, and constructivist. I foster active dialogue and systematic logical reasoning to guide students through complex concepts. In my class, students play an active role in shaping the pace, structure, and depth of our discussions. The core aim of my methodology is to spark wonder, nurture curiosity, and build critical thinking tools for lifelong independent learning.

I take a historical perspective on science, walking students through the evolution of ideas and the processes of scientific discovery. This approach allows them to appreciate the wonder, nuance, and rigor of the scientific method, rather than simply memorizing facts. By engaging with science as a dynamic and evolving discipline, students gain a deeper and more meaningful understanding of the subject.

To keep learning engaging and enriching, I incorporate a variety of resources, including excerpts from science non-fiction books, documentaries, visualizations, YouTube videos, and real-life dialogues. These materials create a multidimensional learning experience and foster exploration beyond the classroom.

I also emphasize the importance of knowledge organization and scope, which are as crucial as acquiring knowledge itself. I introduce students to innovative methods of organizing information, such as creative note-taking, mind maps, and other cognitive tools. Drawing insights from the psychology of learning, I encourage techniques like spaced repetition, mnemonics, and storytelling to help students effectively memorize and retrieve knowledge with ease.

Finally, I integrate the concepts being taught across disciplines such as sociology, arts, and philosophy to emphasize their broader context and relevance. By connecting scientific knowledge to societal, cultural, and philosophical dimensions, I aim to create an embodied learning experience.
