Rooted in Wonder, Dialogue, and Discovery

"The purpose of education is not the transmission of facts, but the transformation of the mind and spirit."

My teaching is rooted in the belief that science is not just a discipline but a dialogue — between humans and nature, past and present, fact and meaning. I embrace a Socratic and constructivist approach, emphasizing deep questions over shallow answers and interconnection over compartmentalization.

I guide students through the evolution of scientific thought, integrating literature, philosophy, and visual storytelling. I encourage them to develop not only competence, but also curiosity and critical thinking — skills essential to becoming independent learners and thoughtful citizens.

In every classroom I strive to create a space where wonder is not incidental but intentional — where learning becomes a form of joyful resistance to mediocrity, and knowledge becomes a tool for transformation.
